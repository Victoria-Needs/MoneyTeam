Tips and Tricks

Protecting yourself from scams

What's even worse then spending money is losing money, make sure you watch out for scams that may try to take advantage of coronavirus. Scams can take any form. Be wary of any unsolicated offers/deals you may recieve that are too good to be true.

Track your spending

The first step to track your psending is to see how much money is exiting your accounts. For two weeks track everything that is leaving through your accounts. Make sure you include EVERY transaction whoever smal it may be. Your bank may already have this produced for you or many banks requrie you to request your spending. This may be challenging and boring for the one or two weeks but will ensure you're on your way to saving in no time.It will give you a clear view of where your money is going and highlight any clear problems you may have.

Seperate utlities from wants

Use the infromation you have collected from tracking your spening to decide which spendings are your 'wants' and those that are the essential utilities that you need to stay, e.g. gas, energy, internet and rent.

Once you have done this start cutting down on your wants. You can also look for cheaper alternatives for your current utilities
